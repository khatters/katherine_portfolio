UX Design @ Hallmark

Role: Digital Merchandising and UX Intern for Hallmark.com

Overview: Hallmark.com is the primary eCommerce platform for Hallmark Cards Inc., a lifestyle company producing cards, gift wrap, party supplies, and a wide range of gift products.

Problem: In early 2024, Hallmark.com launched a new site. However, we saw issues with meeting user journey expectations and the site's main navigation failing to support product understanding and discovery.

Design Process: To tackle this problem, I worked with the UX team to identify user journey patterns (such as shopping first by holiday/occasion, then card recipient, then card format) as well as common roadblocks and navigation misunderstandings (e.g. personalized card vs. 'sign & send' card). Then, I created prototypes that simplified and matched the average user journey.

Outcome: My prototypes passed user testing, and A/B tests on-site will launch before Q4.

Skills Used: User Testing, User Research, Data Analytics, Persona Creation & Ideation, UX Writing, Prototyping, Wireframing, Figma

Role: UX Team Member for Hallmark Intern Group Project

Overview: During my summer internship with Hallmark, I completed a 3-month group project with 6 interns across various departments. My role was to bring our concepts to life as well as connect them to Hallmark's loyalty & rewards program, Crown Rewards.

UX Design @ University of Michigan Museum of Art (UMMA)

Role: Marketing & Web Content Asisstant for UMMA

Overview: The University of Michigan Museum of Art (UMMA) is the city of Ann Arbor's main art museum and one of the largest university art museums in the US. UMMA prides itself on its unique and immersive exhibits, as well as its role as a community gathering space.

Problem: UMMA's 40 foot tall exhibit 'Future Cache,' presented by artist Andrea Carlson, is a work that highlights Cheboiganing (Burt Lake) Band of Ottawa and Chippewa Native Americans who were removed from their land in Michigan. The exhibit is presented both in English and Anishinaabe, a native language. However, without a digital component visitors were unable to hear and experience the Anishinaabe language.

Design Process: To implement the digital component, I worked with the UMMA marketing office to install a QR code in the exhibit that allows users to easily access the digital experience on their phones. Then, I created a prototype that simply and easily highlighted how to listen to both the English and Anishinaabe featured in the exhibit, with the digital experience providing both dialects of Anishinaabe. My design mimics that of the 40-foot wall, so as not to draw the visitors out of the exhibit.

Outcome: My prototype was approved by the marketing office and sent to UMMA's tech partners for production. The digital experience was implemented for the duration of the exhibit (approx. 9 months) and made available on the UMMA website for those unable to visit the exhibit in-person.

Skills Used: Marketing, Prototyping, Wireframing, Figma

UX Design @ UMich School of Information

Role: UX Design Student Focusing on Accessibility

Overview: For my UX Design class SI 422: Needs Assessment & Usability Evaluation, my team was challenged to create a product that solved a student user need. We decided to create an app that would assist mobility impaired students and faculty, making it easier for them to travel around campus, visit dining halls, and find accessible places to park.

Problem: Creating an app that was inclusive of all mobility impairments (e.g., someone with limited finger usage), included the most accurate and useful campus data, and could be used in-tandem with a fully-abled person (i.e., if a mobility-impaired student was traveling with an able-bodied friend).

Design Process: To create the app prototype, I worked with team members who interviewed mobility-impaired students on campus and researched key digital needs for mobility-impaired users. I also worked to make the app fully integrated with existing UofM applications, adhering to the style and usability guidelines.

Outcome: Our team successfully presented our final project and recieved an A for our concept, research, and app design.

Skills Used: Persona Creation and Ideation, User Interviewing, User Research, Prototyping, Wireframing, Figma

UX Design @ MProduct

Role: MProduct Club Member: Product Design

Overview: MProduct is an application-based University of Michigan student organization that supports students who are interested in product development, product management, and product design. Accepted students may elect to choose an area of focus and are then sorted into teams to create a product that's ultimately pitched to the wider MProduct organization.

Problem: The University of Michigan is surrounded by thousands and thousands of restaurants and cafes, however, a frequent complaint on campus is the inability to find new spots that fit with an individual student's limited spending power. My team decided to create an app that helps UofM students find, rate, and explore new restaurants based on price, location, cusine, and relative student ranking.

Design Process: While creating our app prototype, I collaborated with team members to interview students, analyze student needs, and do competative research on other social media platforms and food-finding sites. I aimed to create a fun and lively app experience, vaguely linking it to the University of Michigan brand without creating explicit assocation.

Outcome: Our team successfully pitched our project to the wider MProduct organization; our project was approved to move to the next stage of production.

Skills Used: Persona Creation and Ideation, User Interviewing, User Research, Prototyping, Wireframing, Figma
